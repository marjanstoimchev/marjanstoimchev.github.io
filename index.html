AI Researcher Specializing in Data-Efficient Learning

I am finalizing my PhD in Artificial Intelligence at the Jožef Stefan Institute, where I develop novel, domain-agnostic methods in supervised, semi-supervised, and self-supervised learning. My goal is to reduce reliance on labeled data while delivering robust and generalizable solutions across diverse application areas.

My research spans multiple domains including remote sensing, oncology imaging, and digital pathology, demonstrating that my methods are both transferable and scalable. I have extensive experience in end-to-end deep learning pipelines, from data preprocessing and augmentation to large-scale multi-GPU training, optimization, and evaluation.

While much of my work is grounded in computer vision (CNNs, ViTs, GNNs), I am highly adaptable and passionate about applying my skills to new AI domains, always with a focus on robustness, scalability, and real-world impact.
