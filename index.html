CV

I am a PhD researcher at the Jožef Stefan Institute, where I am advised by Sašo Džeroski and Dragi Kocev. I received my MSc in Electrical Engineering from the University of Ljubljana, where I was awarded the Prešern Award for Best Master's Thesis.

My main research interests are in machine learning and computer vision. I focus on the intersection of remote sensing image analysis, semi-supervised learning, self-supervised learning, and deep learning. My work explores how these areas can complement each other to develop systems capable of efficiently learning rich representations from limited labeled data in geospatial applications.

I am currently completing my PhD and actively seeking AI/ML research opportunities in industry.
Please reach out if you think I could be a good fit!

Selected Projects

Deep Learning for Multiplex Immunofluorescence Imaging

Developing self-supervised models (DinoV2, DinoV3, masked autoencoders) for whole slide image analysis in cancer research, including embedding space analysis, neighborhood detection, image retrieval, clustering algorithms, and transition analysis for clinical applications.

STEM Image Drift Correction

Led development of deep learning solution for drift correction in atomically resolved Scanning Transmission Electron Microscopy images. The approach significantly reduced acquisition time and improved image quality, becoming standard practice in imaging operations.

Hierarchical Multi-label Remote Sensing Classification

Implemented graph neural networks for hierarchical classification of satellite images, combining classical machine learning with deep learning methods to handle complex spatial relationships and taxonomic structures.

Zero-shot Image Segmentation for Nanomagnetic Particles

Developed segmentation methods for analysis of nanomagnetic particles without requiring labeled training data, enabling automated materials analysis workflows.
